# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Health-conscious adults (25–45) wanting fast, friction-free meal logging. The core persona is the casual tracker: logging between meetings, not weighing ingredients to the gram. A secondary persona is the precision macro counter who needs accuracy but still wants the app to stay out of the way.

Primary job: log meals accurately in under 15 seconds, then trust the totals.

## Product Purpose

BinoFit is a local-first nutrition tracker. No account required; no server dependency. It gives users a clear daily picture of calories and macros, logged quickly and understood at a glance. Success is a user who opens the app three times a day, logs in seconds, and closes it feeling informed rather than judged.

## Brand Personality

**Sharp. Precise. Quiet.**

BinoFit is not a motivational app. It doesn't celebrate streaks or shame missed days. It earns trust through accuracy and restraint, not personality. Interactions should be immediate; the interface steps aside so the data takes center stage.

References: Cronometer (data density, information hierarchy, substance-leads-style-follows) and Linear (crisp, no decorative chrome, tool-grade precision).

Emotional goal: users feel in control and informed, never pressured or coached.

## Anti-references

- **Noom / WW**: coaching tone, behavioral-nudge UI, weight-loss-program framing. BinoFit does not moralize food choices.
- **MyFitnessPal**: gamified streaks, notification aggression, wellness pastels, ad-cluttered surfaces.
- Generic health app aesthetic: soft greens, teal accents, rounded-everything, motivational copy.

## Design Principles

1. **Data first** — every UI element serves the information. If it doesn't help the user understand their nutrition picture faster, remove it.
2. **Calm density** — pack information without visual noise. Cronometer earns its density through rhythm and grouping; replicate that discipline.
3. **Trustworthy precision** — the design must feel rigorous. Sharp edges, exact numbers, unambiguous hierarchy. No soft approximations.
4. **Invisible affordance** — interactions feel immediate and obvious. The interface never draws attention to itself.
5. **Non-judgmental neutrality** — color carries meaning only when it signals actionable data, never judgment.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance. Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components. Support for dynamic type scaling and screen readers on iOS and Android.
